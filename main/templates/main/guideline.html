Instructions for the Valence-Arousal labeling task

You are asked to label financial-news sentences along two dimensions: Valence (negative to positive) and Arousal (mild to intense). The image below shows an illustration of how you can visualize these dimensions:

General advise

  • Valence and Arousal are independent dimension. Try to the best of your ability to consider both dimension independently. For instance, a sentence can express very positive or negative sentiment, while being mild (e.g,. consider sentences expressing boredom or serenity).
  • Pay attention to the language of the sentence, rather than inferring about the meaning of the content (including numbers). Do not use any background/contextual information you might be aware of.

Labeling Valence

Guiding question: As an investor, how positive/negative would I view this sentence?

Indicators of high valence (positive/very positive):
  • Words of praise/excitement/thrill.
  • Optimistic attitude.
  • Use of positive words and word modifiers.
  • Associated emotions: happiness, content, excitement, relaxed
Example sentence: 'Comcast Corp, which is seeking regulatory approval for its $45.2 billion bid for Time Warner Cable, reported a betterthanexpected profit last week, citing the highest number of customer additions for its highspeed Internet in six years.'
Indicators of low valence (very negative/negative)
  • Use of negative words and word modifiers.
  • Showing dismissive/pessimistic attitude.
  • Associated emotions: boredom, anger, frustration, depression
Example sentence: 'At the hearing's conclusion, Diogo called Volkswagen's testimony "absolutely unsatisfactory"'.

Labeling arousal

Guiding question: As an investor, how intense/mild do I think this sentence is?

Indicators of high arousal (intense/very intense):
  • strong emotive language
  • high degree of uncertainy/excitement/surprise/fear
  • Use of amplifying words (e.g. extremely, very, extraordinarily, ...)
  • sense of urgency/ immediate action
  • Associated emotions: excitement, anger, awe, anxiety, fear
Example sentence: 'The management shakeup comes a week after the No. 2 U.S. bank by assets reported its biggest quarterly profit in nearly four years as expenses fell to their lowest since the financial crisis.'
Indicators of low arousal (mild)
  • Weak language
  • Language indicates caution/stagnation
  • No indication of dynamism/ movement
  • Associated emotions: contentment, boredom, sadness, melanchony
Example sentence: 'Schoch said the company took such actions in the first quarter, but noted that they were immaterial and declined to give details.'

___________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________

Perspective

Each sentence is about some entity in the equity markets, which could be a company, region or the market itself. Please consider how you perceive the sentiment if you were an investor who already has a stake in the entity. For instance, if a sentence talks about a stock underperforming, view this as negative news and not positive news as a potential buying opportunity.
